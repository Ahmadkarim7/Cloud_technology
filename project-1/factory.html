Types of Capitalism

Laissez-faire Capitalism:
In this subtype, the government plays a minimal role in the economy, allowing businesses to operate with little regulation or intervention.
Free Market Capitalism:
This subtype emphasizes open competition and private ownership of resources and businesses. Prices are determined by supply and demand forces in the market.
Regulated Capitalism:
In regulated capitalism, the government imposes certain regulations and controls on businesses to ensure fair competition, protect consumers, and address market failures.
